Image Feature Diagrams

1. Convexity and Concavity

Convexity: "Outward-bulging". Concavity: "Inward-curving". Solidity: Object's area divided by its convex hull area.

  • Convex object: Solidity ≈ 1.
  • Concave object: Solidity < 1.

2. Aspect Ratio

Aspect Ratio: Ratio of length to width.

  • High aspect ratio: Elongated.
  • Aspect ratio ≈ 1: Circular/square.

3. Form Factor (Circularity)

Form Factor (Circularity): How close a shape is to a perfect circle.

  • Perfect circle: Form factor = 1.
  • Elongated/irregular: Form factor < 1.

4. Perimeter-Area Ratio (P/A Ratio)

Perimeter-Area Ratio (P/A Ratio): Ratio of perimeter to area. Proxy for surface area-to-volume ratio in 2D. A higher P/A ratio indicates a more complex or convoluted boundary *relative to the object's size*.

  • Shape Complexity: For objects of *similar size*, a higher P/A ratio indicates a more complex shape (more indentations, protrusions, or a more elongated form).
  • Size Effect (Same Shape): For objects of the *same shape*, a *larger* object will have a *smaller* P/A ratio. This is because area increases faster than perimeter as an object scales up.
  • Combined Effect: In real-world data, both shape complexity and size influence the P/A ratio. You need to consider both factors when interpreting this value.
